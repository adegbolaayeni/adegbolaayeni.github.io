Featured Projects

01 / PROJECT
Gender Inequality in Education & Economic Development
This project analyzes how gender inequality in education affects Nigeria’s economic development using 43 years of data (1981–2024).

Using an ARDL econometric model, the study finds that while improving gender parity in education is essential for long-term growth, it has a negative short-term impact on GDP. This is not because gender equality is harmful—but because education is an investment that takes time to yield economic returns.

The analysis also reveals a deeper structural issue: Nigeria is not effectively converting female education into labour market productivity, as reflected in persistent female unemployment.

02 / PROJECT
Single Parenting & Academic Performance
This research project investigated the effect of single-parent family structures on the academic performance of 200 secondary school students in Oyo Town, Oyo State, Nigeria, using a descriptive survey design and stratified random sampling.

The study combined descriptive statistics with an independent-samples t-test and Cohen's d effect size analysis to compare outcomes across family types.

03 / PROJECT
Assitive Technology & Inclusive Education
This project explores how assistive technology affects the learning and participation of students with physical disabilities in secondary schools in Oyo State. Using survey data from 150 respondents, the study reveals a harsh reality: assistive technology is largely unavailable in Nigerian schools, despite being widely recognized as effective.

The research highlights systemic issues, including inadequate funding, insufficient teacher training, and infrastructural barriers, while also proposing practical solutions to advance inclusive education in Nigeria.
